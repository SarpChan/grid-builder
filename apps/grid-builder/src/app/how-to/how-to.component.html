Learn about the rest:

Grid Builder is a tool that helps you design and generate CSS grid layouts. It shines when you need to create responsive grids with a lot of moving pieces.

In the navbar you can find a number of valuable buttons. Let's go over them:

Generate

Press the 'generate' button to generate the code for your grid. If generation is not available, check out the two icons next to the button. They detail warnings and errors that you should. Errors need to be fixed, otherwise no generation is possible.

Preview

Preview your grid in its original size, no distractions. It's very easy to break the view with your grid. To improve usability, some values are clamped if they would cause the site to break visually. Preview removes these safeguards.

Preset

With 'preset' you can get started on a working grid quicker. Be careful, though. Selecting a preset will override your current grid.

Download/ Upload

You can download/ upload your grid as a JSON. This should allow you to save your work before delving into new experiments or if you simply want to pick up where you left off later.

Themecolor

Switch between light and dark mode.

Language

Select your language. Currently Grid Builder supports english and german. Want to contribute with a new language? Check out the github

You can reset the entire application by clicking the 'reset' button on the bottom left of the sidebar

Check out how to interact with your grid:

Grids

You can find a list of your current grids below the navbar. Click one to select, drag and drop to change the order. Press the '+' button on the right to add a new grid.

In the sidebar, clicking the sections viewport, grid, alignment, gaps open a menu to edit specific properties of your grid. Visit their respective pages to learn more.

Rows and Columns

You can add or remove rows and columns by clicking '+' or '-' on the respective property at the bottom of the sidebar. The last row/ column will be removed. A new row/ column will be appended at the end.

You can also remove a specific row or column by selecting it and clicking the 'delete' button.

Hover over a row or column to see it light up. Clicking on it will select it. This will open the selection menu in your sidebar. Here you will find further options to edit your selection. For rows and columns you can change the height and width respectively.

Elements

See elements as slots that will receive semantic meaning when we add Areas into the mix. Elements themselves define the region for an area inside a single grid.

By clicking on a cell of a grid, you start creating an element. Click on another cell to finish the creation. Right click or press esc to cancel. Afterwards, the sidebar will once again open to give you further edit options, namely the col/row start and end and which area it is assigned to.

Areas

Areas are a representation of a semantic part of your application. Think of a navbar, footer or a main working area. Areas are cross-grid.

Next to the sidebar, a small column reads 'Areas'. Click the '+' button to create a new area. You can adjust the name and the color for your area in the selection menu. The name your area will be used as a CSS class. Chose wisely.

Assign an area to an element by dragging and dropping the area on the element or by selecting your element and chosing the desired area in the selection menu.

Once an area is assigned to an element, the areas selection menu will display a list of grids to which the area is linked. You can conveniently remove that area from any grid using this list.

Check out how to work with multiple grids:

When a specific grid should be active is determined by media queries.

Press the 'viewport' section of the sidebar to open the viewport menu. The options here are fairly minimal, but enough for most usecases.

In the viewport menu you can change for which media type your grid should be used and more importantly at which width to show your current grid.

A few helpful tips:

  • Stick to a single limiter type. Prefer 'to' as a limiter
  • Prefer 'to' as a limiter.
  • Don't use multiple 'None' limiters. View 'None' like a fallback.

Order of Limiters

  • None
  • To
  • From
  • From/ To
Check out how to use the grid section of the sidebar:

The grid section of the sidebar gives you options to change the auto flow of your grid as well as the height and width of your grid.

Most of the time grids are only used as containers and are supposed to be as large as their children need them to be. If you have a different usecase, you can change that here.

The alignment section of the sidebar allows you to adjust the alignment of your items and the content as a whole.

What's the difference between items and content?

Items refer to the alignment inside a cell whie content aligns all items respective to each other
